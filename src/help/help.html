League Account Switcher

Switch between your Riot / League accounts in a couple of clicks.

How it works

The app reuses Riot's own “Stay signed in” session. For each account it saves an encrypted snapshot of that session. When you switch, it closes the Riot Client, swaps in the saved session, and relaunches — so you land signed in without typing anything. If there's no usable saved session and you stored a password, it can auto-type the login instead.

First time: add & capture an account

  1. Click + Add account. Give it a label (e.g. “Main”), your Riot username, optionally a password, and a region. Save.
  2. Sign in to that account in the Riot Client with “Stay signed in” checked.
  3. Back in this app, click Capture on that account. It closes the Riot Client to lock in a valid session and saves it.
  4. Repeat for each account. After that, switching is one click — or right-click the tray icon.
Never use Riot's “Sign out”. Just close the Riot Client window (or let this app close it). Riot's Sign out button revokes the session on their servers, which makes your saved session useless — the next switch will fall back to a password login or get stuck on the sign-in screen. If that happens, sign in again and press Capture.

What each button does

  • Switch — close the current client and sign in as this account.
  • Capture — save this account's current “Stay signed in” session for fast switching. Closes the Riot Client (needed to save a valid token).
  • Edit — change the label, username, password, or region.
  • Delete — remove the account and its saved session.

Reordering & sections

Drag an account card to reorder it. Create a section to group accounts (e.g. “Main”, “Smurfs”), drag cards into it, and click a section header to collapse it. The same order and grouping is used in the tray right-click menu.

Toolbar buttons

Top-right of the window:

  • Auto Accept — toggle auto-accepting queue pops (green = on, red = off). See below.
  • Appear offline (eye icon) — log in with chat set to offline (green = normal, gray = offline). See below.
  • Porofessor / OP.GG — open the signed-in account's live game / profile on those stats sites (League must be running and signed in).

Auto Accept

Turn on Auto Accept and the app accepts any queue's ready check for you — no matter the queue type. It's a global switch: it stays on until you turn it off, and keeps working even with the window closed to the tray.

Set Auto accept after (in the settings strip) to how long it waits before accepting: 2 seconds by default, 0 = as soon as the pop appears, up to 10 (the client's accept window). It checks more often while you're in matchmaking so the pop is caught quickly, and stays idle otherwise.

Appear offline

The eye button sets your League chat to offline. It's a one-shot toggle tied to your next switch:

  • Turn it on while signed in → the current account goes offline and stays until you switch.
  • Turn it on with no client open → it arms for the first account you switch to, which then logs in offline.
  • Switching while it's on reverts you to online, so the next account logs in normally.

Green = normal/online, gray = appearing offline. It only changes chat presence — nothing else about the account.

Sync settings across accounts

League stores your in-game settings on Riot's servers per account, so your keybinds, camera, mouse and video/audio reset when you switch. Turn on Sync settings across accounts (settings strip) to keep them the same everywhere.

  1. Sign in to the account whose settings you want as your baseline and set everything up the way you like.
  2. Tick Sync settings across accounts — it snapshots your current settings as the baseline. (If no client is running yet, it asks you to log in first so the baseline isn't empty.)
  3. From now on, every switch re-applies that baseline, so each account shows your settings.

Changed your settings and want them everywhere? Click Update baseline to save the current settings as the new baseline. If you click it during a game, it waits until the game ends before saving, so your in-game changes are included.

Heads up. Only keybinds, camera and video/audio (game.cfg, input.ini, PersistedSettings.json) are shared — rune pages and item sets stay per account. If you launch a different account outside this app, the next time you open the switcher it offers to Apply now (or it applies on your next switch anyway).

The tray icon

Right-click the tray icon to see all accounts and switch instantly. Left-click or double-click opens this window. Closing the window keeps the app running in the tray; use Quit in the tray menu to exit fully.

Region & settings

The Default region at the top is pre-filled for new accounts (you can override it per account). Start with Windows launches the app to the tray when you log in to Windows, and Auto update downloads and installs new versions on its own. The rows below hold Auto accept after (the accept delay) and Sync settings across accounts, both covered above.

Sessions expire

Riot's saved sessions last roughly 1–3 weeks. When a card shows “may be expired”, sign in to that account again and press Capture to refresh it. (If you stored a password, switching still works — it just auto-types the login instead of using the fast path.)

Troubleshooting

  • It started typing my login — that's the password fallback. Don't touch the mouse or keyboard while it clicks and pastes.
  • A captcha appeared — solve it yourself in the Riot Client; the switch continues once you're signed in.
  • Stuck on the sign-in screen — the saved session was rejected (often from using Riot's “Sign out”). Sign in manually, then press Capture.
  • “Switching would close it” — you're in champ select or a game. Finish first, or use Force switch to close it anyway.

Your passwords and saved sessions are encrypted with Windows DPAPI — readable only by your Windows user on this PC, and never uploaded anywhere. This app talks only to the Riot Client and League client running locally on your machine.
