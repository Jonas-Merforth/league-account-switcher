League Account Switcher

Switch between your Riot / League accounts in a couple of clicks.

How it works

The app reuses Riot's own “Stay signed in” session. For each account it saves an encrypted snapshot of that session. When you switch, it closes the Riot Client, swaps in the saved session, and relaunches — so you land signed in without typing anything. If there's no usable saved session and you stored a password, it can auto-type the login instead.

First time: add & capture an account

  1. Click + Add account. Give it a label (e.g. “Main”), your Riot username, optionally a password, and a region. Save.
  2. Sign in to that account in the Riot Client with “Stay signed in” checked.
  3. Back in this app, click Capture on that account. It closes the Riot Client to lock in a valid session and saves it.
  4. Repeat for each account. After that, switching is one click — or right-click the tray icon.
Never use Riot's “Sign out”. Just close the Riot Client window (or let this app close it). Riot's Sign out button revokes the session on their servers, which makes your saved session useless — the next switch will fall back to a password login or get stuck on the sign-in screen. If that happens, sign in again and press Capture.

What each button does

  • Switch — close the current client and sign in as this account.
  • Capture — save this account's current “Stay signed in” session for fast switching. Closes the Riot Client (needed to save a valid token).
  • Edit — change the label, username, password, or region.
  • Delete — remove the account and its saved session.

The tray icon

Right-click the tray icon to see all accounts and switch instantly. Left-click or double-click opens this window. Closing the window keeps the app running in the tray; use Quit in the tray menu to exit fully.

Region & settings

The Default region at the top is pre-filled for new accounts (you can override it per account). Start with Windows launches the app to the tray when you log in to Windows.

Sessions expire

Riot's saved sessions last roughly 1–3 weeks. When a card shows “may be expired”, sign in to that account again and press Capture to refresh it. (If you stored a password, switching still works — it just auto-types the login instead of using the fast path.)

Troubleshooting

  • It started typing my login — that's the password fallback. Don't touch the mouse or keyboard while it clicks and pastes.
  • A captcha appeared — solve it yourself in the Riot Client; the switch continues once you're signed in.
  • Stuck on the sign-in screen — the saved session was rejected (often from using Riot's “Sign out”). Sign in manually, then press Capture.
  • “Switching would close it” — you're in champ select or a game. Finish first, or use Force switch to close it anyway.

Your passwords and saved sessions are encrypted with Windows DPAPI — readable only by your Windows user on this PC, and never uploaded anywhere. This app talks only to the Riot Client and League client running locally on your machine.
